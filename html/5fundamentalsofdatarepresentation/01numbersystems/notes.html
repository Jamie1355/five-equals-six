There are six number systems you must be familiar with - natural, integer, rational, irrational, real and ordinal.

Integer numbers ℤ are whole numbers, both positive and negative (no decimals or fractions). ℤ = {..., -3, -2, -1, 0, 1, 2, 3, ...}
Natural numbers ℕ are positive integers including zero. ℕ = {0, 1, 2, 3, ...}
Rational numbers ℚ are numbers which can be written as fractions of integers, e.g. 3/4, 7/1, 43/6.
Irrational numbers are numbers which cannot be written as fractions, e.g. √ 2, π.
Real numbers ℝ are rational or irrational numbers, e.g. 5, 6/7, -17, 0, π.
Ordinal numbers are used to tell the position of things in order, e.g. 1st, 2nd, 5th etc.
